Walter Benjamin

Visionary thinker and writer

"There is no document of civilization that is not at the same time a document of barbarism."

Here's a timeline of Walter Bejamin's life:

  • 1892 - Born in Berlin, Germany into an affluent Jewish family to Emil Benjamin and Pauline Schonflies. His father was a banker in France who settled in Germany and eventually became an antiques trader, owning various businesses.
  • 1912 - Completed secondary school examination and enrolled at the Albert Ludwig University in Freiburg im Breisgau.
  • 1913 - Continued university studies in Berlin at the Humboldt University.
  • 1914 - Elected President of the Berlin "Free Student Group".
  • 18915-17 - Studied at the Ludwig Maximilian University of Munich where he worked on German poet Friedrich Holderlin.
  • 1917 - Married Dora Pollak whom he met at the University of Bern.
  • 1918 - Son, Stefan Rafael, is born.
  • 1920 - Submitted his doctoral thesis on the metaphysics of Johann Gottlieb Fichte and the aesthetics of Friedrich Schlegel.
  • 1921 - Had his first essay published titled ‘Kritik der Gewalt (The Critique of Violence)’.
  • 1924 - Began associating with left-wing intellectuals who helped him to become increasingly interested in a materialistic analysis of the modern world, including Asja Lacis, a Bolshevik actress with whom he had an affair.
  • 1925 - Applied for a position as a professor at the University of Frankfurt, submitting his doctoral thesis as part of his application; because of its unconventional nature, thesis was rejected, and his academic career was ended; Translated the initial volumes of Marcel Proust's 'In Search of Lost Time'.
  • 1926 - Started writing on a regular basis for two German newspapers: ‘Frankfurter Zeitung’ and ‘Die Literarische Welt’. The money from writing for these newspapers was enough for him to live in Paris for some time.
  • 1927-28 - Began work on 'The Arcades Project', a study on 19th century Parisian life and the birth of consumer capitalsm; Published Einbahnstrasse.
  • 1930 - Divorced his wife, Dora.
  • 1932 - When the Nazis came to power, he left Germany and spent some time in Ibiza, Nice, and Sanremo before settling in Paris; worked on ‘A Berlin Chronicle’ and ‘Berlin Childhood around 1900’.
  • 1936 - Published 'The Work of Art in the Age of Mechanical Reproduction'.
  • 1938 - Stripped of his German citizenship as all the German Jews were, at that time. He was then arrested by the French government and was put in a prison camp near Nevers, in Central Burgundy for 3 months.
  • 1940 - When Germans occupied France, attempted to flee to the United States via Spain; arrived safely in Spain but was told he would be repatriated in France; Committed suicide with morphine pills in Portbou, Spain.

"Work on a good piece of writing proceeds on three levels: a musical one, where it is composed; an architectural one, where it is constructed; and finally, a textile one, where it is woven."
